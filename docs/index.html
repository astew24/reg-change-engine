What changed in U.S.
federal regulations today?

reg-change-engine downloads the U.S. Federal Register's daily bulk XML, computes a word-level diff against the previous business day, and classifies every changed paragraph into one of 11 regulatory domains using a zero-shot DistilRoBERTa model.

The result is a structured, queryable feed of exactly which words were added, removed, or modified across every federal rule — useful for compliance teams, policy researchers, and anyone who needs to know what regulators changed before someone else does.

New here? Read this first.

The Federal Register is the U.S. government's daily journal of every new rule, proposed rule, and notice published by federal agencies (EPA, SEC, CMS, etc.). Every business day, it publishes thousands of paragraphs of regulatory text.

This project diffs today's publication against yesterday's at the word level — surfacing every single token that was added, removed, or modified. Each changed paragraph is then classified into a regulatory domain (e.g. environmental, financial, healthcare) so you can filter the feed to whatever you actually care about.

The dashboard below is what a daily summary looks like. Below the metrics you'll find the actual change records, a token-level diff example, classifier performance per domain, and the API you'd query to integrate this feed into your own systems.

How a single day flows through the pipeline

~3 min end-to-end on a laptop

Five stages: download today's XML, diff against yesterday's cached copy, classify the changed paragraphs, write structured records to Postgres, serve them via FastAPI. Each stage is in its own module so it's easy to test or swap.

Quick glossary

terms used on this page
Federal Register
The official daily journal of the U.S. government, where every federal agency publishes new and proposed regulations.
Bulk XML
The full text of a single day's Federal Register, served as one downloadable XML file. The whole-document atomicity is what makes day-over-day diffing reliable.
diff_type
How a paragraph changed: added (new this publication), removed (gone from this publication), or modified (text changed in place).
Token-level diff
Comparison at the word level, not just the paragraph or document level — so you can highlight the exact words that changed inside an otherwise unchanged sentence.
Zero-shot NLI
A classifier that maps text to candidate labels using natural-language entailment, without any labeled training data. Lets the project work on day one with no manual annotation.
domain_score
The model's confidence in its top-predicted domain, between 0 and 1. Predictions below the configurable min_confidence are returned as "other" rather than confidently mislabeled.
